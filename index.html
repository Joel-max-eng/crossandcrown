🚲 Cycle of Hope

Empowering Girls to Ride Boldly into Their Future

Cycle of Hope is a transformative initiative designed to empower and engage girls through a dynamic blend of faith, skill-building, and fun. Rooted in discipleship and driven by purpose, the program keeps girls actively involved in Bible sessions, life skills workshops, and cycling training — helping them build healthy habits, overcome fear, and make wise choices.

By staying engaged in positive activities, girls learn to resist negative peer pressure, including early sexual activity, and stay focused on their education and personal growth.

🌄 More Than a Ride

  • 💪 Confidence
  • 🧠 Resilience
  • 💜 Values that guide them both on and off the bike — including perseverance, courage, and determination

🎯 Our Goal

We aim to empower 300 girls by the end of 2027, each one supported through our Sponsorship Program — from early childhood all the way to university.

  • 🚴 Our pilot project begins with 30 girls from the slums, with an additional 30 expected to join by the end of August
  • 🤝 Each participant will be matched with a mentor or guide committed to walking with her for at least three years
  • 📖 Together, we build a foundation of discipleship, mentorship, and mobility

🙌 How You Can Help

We are currently in need of:

  • 30 mountain bikes for girls aged 8 to 13
  • Protective gear including helmets and guards
  • Financial support to expand our reach and sustain our programs

🌸 Alabaster Voices

"Every girl has a story. Every instrument tells a story."

Alabaster Voices is a ministry born from a deep burden: to restore dignity, hope, and purpose to vulnerable girls through worship, music, and discipleship. Inspired by the woman in the Gospels who poured out her alabaster jar at Jesus’ feet, we believe every girl carries a precious treasure within her — a gift waiting to be poured out for God’s glory.

Though society rejected her, Jesus honored her. He said, “She has done a beautiful thing to me,” and promised her story would be remembered wherever the gospel is preached. Today, many girls live that same story — abandoned, silenced, or told they don’t matter. But we see what Jesus sees: beauty, purpose, and a voice worth hearing.

🎶 Our Vision

  • Discover and nurture their musical talents
  • Learn to play instruments and lead worship
  • Shine boldly in public spaces as vessels of healing and transformation
  • Restore dignity and hope to those who feel forgotten
  • Mentor them spiritually, emotionally, and practically
  • Empower them to live boldly for Jesus

🎺 How You Can Help

We are currently seeking donations of gently used musical instruments to help launch and expand this ministry. Specifically, we need:

  • Brass: Trumpets, Trombones, Tubas, Cornets, Euphoniums
  • Woodwind: Flutes, Clarinets, Saxophones
  • Strings & Keys: Guitars, Pianos
  • Percussion: Drums

Your gift can help a girl find her voice, reclaim her story, and rise in worship. Just like the alabaster jar, what you pour out will become a beautiful offering remembered for generations.

Buying musical instruments in Kenya is often costly, but every donation helps us reach more girls with the tools they need to shine. Whether you give a little or a lot, your gift becomes part of a girl’s story — a story of dignity, worship, and transformation.

Every instrument becomes a vessel of worship. Every gift becomes a seed of hope.

🎭 Puppet Parables – Program Overview

🌟 Mission Statement

“Engaging Bible storytelling through puppetry — making Scripture fun, interactive, and unforgettable for children and youth.”

📖 Our Story

Puppet Parables was born out of a passion to make God’s Word come alive for children.

While serving with YWAM England in the children’s ministry department, my son Jeremy and I received a scholarship from One Way UK and Kings Kids England to attend a three-day national Puppet Festival. This event brought together some of the best puppeteers from across the UK, offering world-class training and workshops.

It was there that we learned how to deliver engaging Bible stories through puppets, create interactive shows, and even explored advanced techniques like blacklight (UV) puppetry.

God confirmed the vision He had already placed in my heart — to use puppetry as a creative tool to reach children.

🎪 Today, Puppet Parables seeks to:

  • 🎓 Bring puppetry to schools as part of assemblies and life-skills programs
  • 🎪 Host Bible clubs where children not only watch but also learn to perform with puppets
  • 📱 Create puppet shows for social media, reaching children and families everywhere
  • 🙌 Train children and youth to use puppetry as a form of evangelism, discipleship, and creative expression

💡 Why Puppet Parables?

Children learn best when they are engaged. Puppets grab attention, break down walls, and make learning fun.

  • The Bible becomes relatable and interactive
  • Children participate, not just listen
  • Hard lessons are made gentle and memorable
  • It creates safe spaces for kids to laugh, ask questions, and grow in faith

🎯 How We Deliver It

  • 🏫 School Assemblies – Short, impactful puppet shows with Bible lessons
  • 🎭 Weekly Puppet Clubs – Where kids learn puppetry skills and rehearse skits
  • 🌍 Community Outreach – Puppet shows in villages, streets, and churches
  • 📺 Online Kids Shows – Short puppet skits on social media to teach God’s Word

💛 Our Request for Support

We invite you to partner with us to bring the joy of Puppet Parables to thousands of children in Kenya.

You can support by:

  • Donating via the “Give” button on our website
  • Sending us an email through our Contact Us form
  • Volunteering as a puppeteer or trainer

Together, we can raise a generation of children who love the Bible — and love sharing it with others.

🌱 SDGs Disciples: Small Disciples Growing

🌟 Vision

To raise a generation of Christ-like children and youth who love God, love people, and care for creation — living out biblical truth in every area of life through the lens of the Sustainable Development Goals.

🎯 Mission

SDG Disciples empowers children and young people aged 7 to 18 to live out their faith practically by learning about the world’s needs, embracing God’s Word, and taking action — goal by goal. Through Bible storytelling, Scripture-based teaching, mentorship, creative worship, and hands-on activities, we inspire young disciples to be agents of change in their communities and beyond.

📌 What We Do

  • Bible Storytelling & Lessons: Connecting Bible stories with real-life challenges
  • SDG Learning: Teaching about the 17 Sustainable Development Goals in kid-friendly ways
  • Practical Action: Organizing projects like clean-ups, food drives, and awareness campaigns
  • Mentorship & Worship: Encouraging spiritual growth and leadership
  • Events & Camps: Fun and impactful gatherings for children and youth

🤝 Get Involved

Parents, churches, and schools are invited to partner with us! Whether you want your child to join, volunteer to help, or support our work, there’s a place for you here.

  1. Join a group near you
  2. Volunteer with us
  3. Donate to support our projects

📘 Sample Lesson Plan for SDGs Disciples

WEEK 1: Love Your Neighbour – The Good Samaritan

Session: Innami Education Centre, 20th December
Target group: Grade 6 students (class size: 35)
Facilitator: Meshack

Bible Passage: Luke 10:25–37
SDG Focus: SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities), SDG 16 (Peace, Justice & Strong Institutions)

Jesus teaches us to love and care for others, even if they are different from us. Everyone is our neighbor.

🧠 Memory Verse

“Love your neighbor as yourself.” – Mark 12:31

🧩 Activities

  • Heart Craft: Make paper hearts with ways to show kindness.

  • Who was the good neighbor in the story?
  • Why is it important to help those in need?
  • Do we treat everyone fairly and kindly?

🗓 Take-Home Challenge

Do one kind act this week for someone who is not your close friend or is different from you.

🙏 Prayer

“Lord, help us to love everyone like You do. Teach us to be kind and fair to all. Amen.”

👧 Walk With Her

👣 Walk With Her x 🚲 Cycle of Hope
A Mentorship Journey in Motion

Walk With Her is our heart-centered mentorship program designed to walk alongside girls from marginalized communities with dignity, discipleship, and direction. Through life skills, encouragement, camps, and career guidance, we help girls discover their identity, build resilience, and dream boldly.

Now, through our partnership with Cycle of Hope, this journey takes on new momentum.

Each Walk With Her mentee receives a bicycle, becoming the first participants in Cycle of Hope — a ministry that blends faith, skill-building, and fun activities to keep girls engaged, focused, and flourishing.

🌸 Walk With Her Activities:

  • Life Skills Sessions
  • Bible clubs and crafts facilitated by staff and volunteers. Girls learn from heroes in the Bible like Esther, Mary, Hannah, and many more.
  • Encouragement Calls from mentors and disciplers
  • Walk With Her Camps with workshops, games, sports, crafts, and discipleship
  • One-on-One Mentorship with trained volunteers
  • Match & Connect with career role models and guides

🚲 Cycle of Hope Integration:

  • Weekly cycling drills and training
  • Bible sessions and life skills workshops
  • Positive engagement to build healthy habits and wise choices
  • Protection against negative peer pressure, including early sexual activity

💜 Our Vision

To see every girl rise above fear, reclaim her voice, and move forward — spiritually, emotionally, and physically.

Tagline:
"She is mentored. She is mobile. She is moving forward in faith."

⚽ Introducing KICK Academy

KICK Academy is a dynamic ministry that transforms young lives through sport, support, and spiritual formation. By blending high-quality football and dance coaching with values-based mentorship, KICK creates spaces where movement meets meaning — and every child is invited to grow in confidence, character, and faith.

We’re proud to partner with KICK Academy as part of our ministry expression, bringing this powerful model into schools and communities that need it most.

🎯 Outreach Launch

Innami & House of Hope – December 2025
Our first outreach begins at Innami Education Centre in Kitale, Kenya — a school serving nearly 400 children, with the majority being girls from the surrounding slums of Misemwa, Forkland, Matisi, and Mitume. These girls face immense challenges, often dropping out of school due to poverty and neglect. We believe they are pivotal to community transformation — and we’re committed to walking with them.

We’re launching with 50 girls in December, and hope to expand to 200 girls across Innami and the nearby House of Hope Academy, once our partnership is confirmed.

👧 Why Girls?

In many communities, the boy child receives more attention and support, while girls are overlooked. We’re changing that narrative. Our outreach is intentionally designed to affirm, equip, and empower girls — helping them discover their God-given potential through play, purpose, and partnership.

⚽ What We Offer

  • Football & dance coaching linked to transformational values
  • Life discussions that connect sport to spiritual growth
  • Inclusive, safe spaces for personal development
  • Church partnerships to sustain long-term impact

🌟 Founder, Cross and Crown
Emmanuel Godfrey Ogoma

Emmanuel Godfrey Ogoma, together with his wife Joyce, is the visionary founder of Cross and Crown, a ministry rooted in compassion, creativity, and Christ-centered discipleship. His journey began in 2010 at the Youth With A Mission (YWAM) base in Arusha, Tanzania, where he studied Principles in Children and Youth Ministry (PCYM). It was there that Emmanuel first discovered his deep calling to serve children and young people.

After graduating, Emmanuel moved to Mityana District in Uganda, serving as a missionary for two years. Alongside Joyce, he ministered to children and youth with love, dignity, and transformative care. Their impact was profound—planting seeds of hope in young lives and nurturing spiritual growth through relational outreach.

In 2012, the couple returned to Kenya and founded Global Steps, a community-based organization committed to providing educational opportunities for underprivileged children in Kitale. Through Global Steps and now Circle of Love, over 700 children have gained access to education, with many graduates advancing to college and university. Emmanuel’s leadership continues to open doors for future generations.

Emmanuel’s training includes:

  • Discipleship Studies at YWAM Athi River
  • Business Administration and Management – Cambridge International College
  • Youth Work Certification – focus on mentoring and discipling young girls

His vision is clear: to see girls and women live fruitful lives, rooted in their identity in Christ and empowered to pursue their divine calling.

Emmanuel and Joyce are blessed with three sons—Jeremy, Joshua, and Jayden—who inspire their commitment to family and ministry.

Emmanuel’s global experience includes serving at YWAM Kings Lodge in the UK, where he worked in the children’s department for two years. During this time, he received specialized training in:

  • Basic Puppetry with One Way UK
  • Sports Discipleship with KICK
  • Open the Book – a creative Bible storytelling program using skits and drama for children’s assemblies

These creative tools have enriched his ministry approach, enabling him to disciple children through sports, music, drama, and dance. Emmanuel is passionate about bringing these ideas home to Kenya, where they continue to spark joy, faith, and transformation among the youth.

Empowering Girls Through Movement, Mission, and Ministry

We’re delighted to announce the official launch of KICK Academy—a bold new initiative designed to uplift and empower girls through sport, creativity, and faith. This December, Innami Education Centre will host a joyful celebration marking the beginning of a transformative journey for our first cohort of girls.

📅 December 20th
🕗 Time: 8 AM – 12 Noon
👧 Target Group: Girls aged 10 to 15 years
🙌 Facilitators: Emmanuel & Chris
🎥 Outreach coordinator: Meshack M

🌟 What to Expect:

  • Soccer & Dance Sessions – Energetic, expressive, and full of joy—girls will engage in movement that builds confidence, teamwork, and celebration.
